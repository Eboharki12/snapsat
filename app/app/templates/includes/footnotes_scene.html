1. Remapping segments of the electromagnetic spectrum we normally can't see into one of the red, green, or blue wavelengths, gives us a means of visualizing what would otherwise be invisible. In short, Landsat gives us superpowers.

    Snapsat enables you to do this by composing one image from three Landsat acquired images.

  2. Landsat specifies the wavelengths of light over which it captures images as a band.

    Specific band combinations are useful for illuminating natural and man-made Earth features. Some are just fun to look at. Visit our guide for explanation of the most commonly used combinations.

    The bar code above each preview represents the bands mapped to each of the red, green, and blue bands.

    • LXSPPPRRRYYYYDDDGSIVV
    • L = Landsat
    • X = Sensor
    • S= Satellite
    • PPP = WRS path
    • RRR = WRS row
    • YYYY = Year
    • DDD = Julian day of year
    • GSI = Ground station identifier
    • VV = Archive version number
