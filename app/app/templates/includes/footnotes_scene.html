1. Taking segments of the electromagnetic spectrum that we normally can't see and remapping them into one of the red, green, or blue wavelengths, gives us a means of visualizing what would otherwise be invisible. In short, Landsat gives us superpowers.

    Snapsat enables you to do this by composing three images acquired by Landsat into one.

  2. Landsat specifies the wavelengths of light over which it captures images as a series of bands.

    Specific combinations of bands are useful for illuminating natural and man-made Earth features. Some are just fun to look at. Visit our guide for explanation of the most commonly used combinations.

    The bar code above each preview is a simple visualization representing which bands you have mapped to each of the red, green, and blue bands.

    • LXSPPPRRRYYYYDDDGSIVV
    • L = Landsat
    • X = Sensor
    • S= Satellite
    • PPP = WRS path
    • RRR = WRS row
    • YYYY = Year
    • DDD = Julian day of year
    • GSI = Ground station identifier
    • VV = Archive version number
