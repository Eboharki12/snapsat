Natural color images are created using the 4, 3, and 2 bands of the Landsat 8 satellite. Natural color images appear 'natural', because each of the bands that they use correspond to the colors we are naturally able to perceive, Red, Green, and Blue.

The combination of the 7, 6, and 4 bands is particularly useful for visualizing urban environments as it uses both the available Short-wave infrared (SWIR) bands. Because the SWIR bands capture longer wavelengths, they're less frequently affected by the smog and haze that typifies urban environments.

The combination of the 5, 4, and 3 bands are useful for analyzing vegetation health. Typically, the healthier the plant, the brighter the red it produces.
